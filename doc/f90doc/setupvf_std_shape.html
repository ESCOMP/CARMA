This routine evaluates particle fall velocities, vf(k) [cm s^-1] and reynolds' numbers based on fall velocities, re(j,i,k) [dimensionless]. indices correspond to vertical level , bin index , and aerosol group .

Non-spherical particles are treated through shape factors and .

General method is to first use Stokes' flow to estimate fall ! velocity, then calculate reynolds' number, then use "y function" (defined in Pruppacher and Klett) to reevaluate reynolds' number, from which the fall velocity is finally obtained.

This routine requires that vertical profiles of temperature , air density , and viscosity are defined (i.e., initatm.f must be called before this).
